## SHOULD fix

- **Word count short.** ≈7463 vs 8000–12000. ~540 words under the floor.
  Not a structural hole — the scenes are complete — but the book's own
  `style.md` ("spend richness generously"; sensory-premise book) wants the
  band hit. Cheapest add-back: one more dwelling moment in the **era** scene
  (the only beat where the sensory premise — color — is *absent*; see below),
  or a few more lines of texture at the pozo.

- **Mild flash-forward at the chapter close.** L203: *"No tenía nombre para
  lo que bajaba. **Mañana lo tendría.** Esta noche solo era un peso nuevo en
  el aire… mientras la aldea dormía sin saberlo."* "Mañana lo tendría" is the
  narrator stepping ahead of Bruno's present knowledge to pre-announce a later
  weight (the named 8d-b tic). It leaks no *content* of the ch2 reveal, so it
  is not a MUST — but decisions-ch01 explicitly wants this ending to be
  "intuición/atmósfera, NO trama." Cutting "Mañana lo tendría" (let the unease
  stand un-promised) keeps it pure atmosphere.

- **El-apagado edges toward naming its cause.** L103: *"como si en algún
  sitio muy lejos alguien estuviera bajando muy despacio una mecha que nadie
  veía y que daba luz a todo."* The image points at a single distant source
  being turned down — i.e. the Vitral bleeding, whose cause (#10) is reserved
  for Book II (shadow: el-apagado caps at `suspected`, "solo se ve la caída,
  no la causa"). It is framed as Bruno's wordless image ("Bruno no tenía
  palabras para eso"), so it stays `sensed`-safe, but it is the one line that
  gestures at mechanism rather than loss. Consider making the image about
  *less* (a lamp running out) rather than *someone lowering a wick* (an agent,
  a cause).

## CONSIDER

- **Aphoristic opening line.** L3: *"La madera decía la verdad antes que la
  boca de nadie."* Reads like a quote-book maxim (the 8d-a watch-item). It is
  past-tense and immediately grounded ("Bruno lo había aprendido con las
  manos…"), so it earns itself and is not the free-floating timeless-present
  ban. Kept as taste-level: strong as a hook, just be aware it is the boldest
  maxim in the chapter.

- **Isolated single-sentence beats recur.** "Listo." (L41), "El pozo no tenía
  brocal." (L61), "Pero las manchas eran pálidas." (L91), "Alargó la mano y lo
  tocó." (L183). Each lands well; together they are a slight rhythm tic (the
  "toll the bell" structural anti-pattern). Five is within tolerance — flagged
  only so a sixth in revision doesn't push it over.

- **The era scene has no color.** Every other scene makes the sensory premise
  (color) felt — the pale vidriera stains on his arms, the graying green
  thread, the candil's yellowing of the room. The threshing scene (L111-151)
  is rich in sound/smell/heat but colorless (the dorado del polvo aside).
  Since this is the book whose engine is color, one beat of color-loss in the
  era — even the grano or the evening light read against memory — would make
  the apagado total. Optional, but it would also recover word count.

## What works

- **Character through action, zero exposition.** The whole "asa de cubo"
  identity is *enacted*, never stated: the Mosén Quirino threshing beat
  (L125-149) is a flawless dramatization of "esconder lo que uno hace *bien*"
  — Bruno deliberately mis-places the gavilla so the man's attention drops
  from his face. Exactly the mundane-friction gate decisions-ch01 asked for.
- **la-mirada-que-sube planted as a bodily habit, not a complaint.** "Le
  había subido los ojos del serón a la cara… Lo estaba viendo." (L133) sets
  up the spine image cleanly for its ch6/ch23 arc.
- **Seeds buried in what the scene already did.** The father (L45-47, L67
  "con las dos") reads as inherited character, not mystery; the telar/hilo
  verde (L185) reads as domestic grief, not a clue — a first-read reader
  cannot state its real meaning, which is the test.
- **Sensory craft is concrete and world-specific.** The wood "talking" out of
  tune (L9), the calce drinking water with a "chasquidito… fino como el de la
  sal en el fuego" (L37), the well-water tasting "a moneda" (L73) — the world
  is felt, not named. This is the texture the style guide asks to be spent.
- **Ending delivers the beat-sheet transition.** "Algo bajaba por el camino…
  hacia Vega Parda" (L201) is precisely the "cambio en el aire" hook to ch2,
  and stays atmosphere over plot (bar the one "Mañana lo tendría" flagged
  above).
