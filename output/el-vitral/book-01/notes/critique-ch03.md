> One MUST-fix cluster: the **aphoristic-narrator tic** — the book's
> single explicit banned prose rule (`style.md`), elevated to MUST in
> `notes/voice.md` after it recurred in ch1 and ch2. It returned again
> here (several instances, several introduced by the expand pass). Per
> the project's own gating rule this blocks lock-in until fixed. It is
> fully polish-fixable (anchor each maxim to Yedra's body/gesture);
> `revise-chapter --mode polish` is the intended tool.

## MUST fix

- **[anti-pattern: aphoristic narrator — único MUST de prosa del libro]**
  Universal maxims in timeless present / closing epigrams. Anchor each to
  Yedra (a gesture, an object, her own thought), do not let the narrator
  climb up to generalize. Clear instances:
  - **"Así se enseña un oficio: se le presta a alguien tus manos hasta
    que le crecen las suyas."** — textbook closing epigram on the
    Tello-teaching paragraph. Re-anchor to her hands on his, drop the
    "así se enseña un oficio" frame.
  - **"El fardo no pesaba al salir; el peso de un fardo no es el de la
    primera cuesta sino el de la última."** — calendar-quote maxim
    (introduced in EXPAND, road scene). Keep the observation, kill the
    aphoristic shape ("a la media hora el fardo ya le tiraba de la
    nuca…").
  - **"Una cara golpeada se cura sola."** — terse universal maxim
    (return scene). Move it onto her body/decision ("no se miró… la cara
    se curaría; le daba igual cómo le quedara").
  - Borderline craft-generalizations in the same family (also from
    EXPAND) — tidy in the same pass so the tic doesn't survive:
    "**tejer es de la mano y del oído antes que del ojo**"; "**hay que
    encontrarle a cada piedra la cara y el peso, probarla en su sitio,
    descartarla si baila…**" (the named "para hacer X hay que Y"
    pattern); "**tirar de un nudo en la tela es llevarse medio palmo de
    trabajo**." Each is fixable by attaching it to what Yedra's hands are
    doing right then, instead of stating the rule.

## SHOULD fix

- **[seed echo: los-huecos — repeated verbatim, not echoed]** Cosme is
  rendered almost word-for-word as Senén (ch1): "andaba… por el medio…
  sin apartarse", "el sol le caía encima sin entrarle", the **red-cloth /
  blue-cloth test** ("le había puesto delante el rojo… y el azul… los
  había mirado igual"), and **"una nube fina cruzando el sol"** behind
  the eyes — all near-identical to ch1. `seed-craft.md`: "Echo in a
  different sensory register… Repeating seeds verbatim trains the reader
  to notice the seed as machinery." The systemic sameness is the point,
  but deliver Cosme through **Yedra's** lens (the weaver who reads by
  hands/cloth) rather than re-using Bruno's exact ch1 images. E.g. let
  her register his grisness by his *hands* on his knees, or by a paño she
  knows he can no longer tell apart — not by re-running the same test.

- **[hard-magic: cost not visible on the magenta]** The magenta flares
  his aura repeatedly and hard ("subió el aura un último grado, todo lo
  suyo junto, para reventarle el aguante"), but pays no visible thermal
  price, while ch2 established **"forzar quema"** (the rojo sweating
  chorros, the Blanco Falso). A push this hard should show a cost — a
  sheen at the temple, a flush under the magenta. Bonus: showing him
  straining makes the **flicker** more legible (he is pushing with
  everything and it guts out) without explaining the mechanism.

- **[lean word count]** 8093 is barely over the floor and the chapter has
  only one true hinge (the market). If `revise` trims the aphorisms it
  may dip under 8000; budget a small dwell (Yedra's hands / the loom at
  night, or the market press) to stay in range rather than padding.

## CONSIDER

- **Rumination length (scenes 4→5 + road).** The realization "el único
  sitio donde el señor no llegaba… y el sitio era ella" is circled three
  times (market aftermath, the repecho, the night loom). It lands the
  first time. Consider trimming one pass so she *intuits* and the reader
  leans in, rather than the chapter underlining it. (Watch this doesn't
  drift toward telling her own thesis.)

- **Fragment-toll ending ("A la vista.").** Strong here — it turns the
  finished, closed tela back on the hidden cabo. It is the
  "isolated dramatic sentence as a bell" pattern (`antipatterns.md`),
  but this is the book's *first* use, so it earns it. Just don't reach
  for the one-line-toll close again in ch4–5.

- **"No se vende lo que no se ha vendido."** Reads a touch maxim-like,
  but it's *dialogue* (her flat logic), so it's in-character — keep.

## What works

- **Yedra's voice is distinct and earned.** Tool-mind, measures the world
  by hands, grief gone cold into a "cosa fría y despierta" with the shape
  of one word, *quién*. She reads people, cloth, stone, and a magenta's
  hand the same way. Clean separation from Bruno's register.
- **The seed plant (`complementario-verde-magenta`) is exemplary.**
  Embedded inside the tithe dispute (the scene's ostensible purpose is the
  cobro, not the flicker), no flag word, the magenta covers with rage, and
  Yedra *feels a correlation she can't name* — never the mechanism.
  Honors shadow Truth 8 (intuye ch3, prueba ch22).
- **The Tello cesto** seeded (the round canillas, scene 1) and paid
  (she finally spends them into the cloth, scene 5) entirely through the
  object — no stated grief. The brother's age-rhyme with Bruno is left
  for the reader to feel.
- **Colour is felt, not named:** magenta as a rose heat "que no calentaba,
  que sólo apretaba" (consistent with ch2), the flicker as a cold *soplo*
  in the palmo of contact, `verde de oír crecer` carried by *weight* in
  the hand. Premise-as-sensation is well met.
- **The thematic engine** arrives as her own craft-metaphor: a tela, however
  tight, can be unravelled if you find the cabo — first time the green
  subplot's logic is on the page, and it comes from the weaver, not the
  narrator.
