# Seeds — El Apagado (Libro I)

These are the foreshadowing seeds for the whole book. Status progresses as
chapters are written: `planned → planted → echoed-N → paid_off`.

**NEVER compress or summarize this file.** It is consulted on every chapter.

> Each seed has: a stable id, a visible detail, a real meaning, the
> chapter to plant in, chapters to echo in, the chapter to pay off in,
> a Trigger (for event payoffs), a Dose (telegraph budget), and — for
> emotional/sensory through-lines — a Resolution image. Use
> `references/seed-craft.md` for craft guidance.
>
> **Two layers:** seeds that pay off **within Libro I** (internal), and the
> trilogy's **obligatory loaded guns** (bible §14) that plant here and pay off
> in Libro III. For the latter, `Payoff in` names the Libro-I touch that
> discharges the concept; the full payoff is noted as Libro III.
>
> **Distribution check:** plants concentrated in act 1 / early act 2
> (ch 1-3, 7-11); payoffs cluster in act 2B / act 3 (ch 13, 15, 19, 20, 22,
> 25). No seed plants and pays in adjacent chapters except where the Dose
> field declares a deliberate near-term detonation (crisol-tensionado).

---

## SEED: aceleracion-color
**Detail:** the world's color is visibly thinning — fields less green, a chapel window paler than its old panels, banners washed out next to old ones.
**Real meaning:** the cicatriz has crossed a perceptual threshold; the world is draining toward the void. This is the causal "why now" — it made Bruno possible and is closing the net on him (bible §13).
**Plant in:** 1
**Echo in:** 5, 18
**Payoff in:** Libro III (the reader understands the loss is irreversible). Libro-I touch: the flat bell of ch 19 (see campanas-hora-color) lets the reader *hear* the drain.
**How to plant:** as background melancholy of a poor province — never flagged. A green coaxing a sick crop; the chapel's blue that "no longer cools like it used to." One quiet line.
**How to pay off:** (Libro III) the reader realizes the pallor was the world dying. In Libro I, only the echo and the bell.
**Dose:** wide gap → 1 plant + 2 oblique echoes. Never named as "the Aceleración" in-scene; it is felt, not explained.
**Status:** planted

## SEED: madre-telar-color
**Detail:** Bruno's warmest memory — his dead mother smoothing a knot with her thumb at her loom, a thread of a specific warm green ("verde de oír crecer") he loved. The loom now stands still.
**Real meaning:** his mother was the surviving Negra (a weaver); she died greying out, vaciada, as Bruno will. The warm-colored memory is exactly what the void erases first. The emotional spine of the erosion arc.
**Plant in:** 1
**Echo in:** 5 (warm, intact), 7 (intact — baseline), 11 (first faint cost), 17 (grey gap), 19 (thread almost grey, song without voice), 21 (less than before)
**Payoff in:** 25 (her face / the thread's color goes grey as he flees)
**Trigger:** the irreversible void-uses — ch 13 (the voice goes) and ch 25 (the face goes). Each "honest" use is the intrinsic cause; never an external memory-thief.
**How to plant:** embed in domestic texture, not as foreshadow. The thumb on the knot, the warm green, the stilled loom. Let the reader love it before it costs anything.
**How to pay off:** at the climax flight, Bruno reaches for her face and finds grey where the color was — the same image as ch 1, inverted. Do not explain; let the loom go grey.
**Dose:** wide gap, resolution-type → plant warm, hold warm through act 1, dim by degrees (one notch per echo). The erosion must be *gradual and measurable* so ch 25 lands.
**Resolution image:** the warm green thread / the loom (ch 1, alive and warm) → the same loom and thread gone grey, the face unreachable (ch 25). The cold/empty where color was.
**Status:** planted

## SEED: ser-funcion-no-persona
**Detail:** eyes that read Bruno's caste/color and his hands, then slide off his face without seeing him; people relate to what he is *for*, never to him.
**Real meaning:** Bruno's Need is to be seen as a person, not a function; his Wound is a world that reduces everyone to color. The thematic spine. The cruelty is that even the "kind" mentor reads him as a thesis.
**Plant in:** 1
**Echo in:** 2 (mentor measures), 4 (caste etiquette), 6, 8 (Vela's fingers-before-face), 16 (the watching classroom), 17, 20 (deepens), 21 (bottom)
**Payoff in:** 25 (inversion)
**How to plant:** a coin taken without a glance; an eye that drops to his brown-marked hands and never rises. Single lines, never flagged.
**How to pay off:** the inversion — Bruno's need is met not by being seen but by *seeing*: he chooses to stay and protect the mentor (a person, flawed and human), acting as a person rather than a function for the first time.
**Dose:** thematic through-line → many light touches across the book, none heavy. The payoff is an action (the choice), not a speech.
**Resolution image:** the eye that drops to the hands and slides off the face (planted ch 1) → Bruno's own gaze finally resting on the mentor *as a person* and choosing him (ch 25).
**Status:** planted

## SEED: portaluz-coreografia
**Detail:** the Portaluz's public image always shows the same improbable fold of robe — identical on a worn coin, a roadside shrine, the public spectacle. (bible §14)
**Real meaning:** the "saviour" is a manufactured political decoy, choreographed and reproduced; he has not been cooked yet and is not what he seems.
**Plant in:** 2
**Echo in:** 12 (in scene), 18 (the great apparition)
**Payoff in:** Libro III (the reader understands the saviour was a decoy). Libro-I touch: Yedra recognizing the plaster under the gold in ch 18.
**How to plant:** Bruno notices the fold on a coin, then on a shrine, and dismisses it as iconography. No flag.
**How to pay off:** (Libro III) the decoy is exposed. In Libro I, Yedra's contempt seeds the reader's suspicion.
**Dose:** wide gap → 1 plant + 2 echoes, each in a different register (coin → live spectacle → crowd's-eye view). Never call it fake in narration.
**Status:** planted (plant landed ch 1 via the coin — one chapter early; ch 2 shrine is now its first echo)

## SEED: campanas-hora-color
**Detail:** the hour-bells of the luz-world are each tuned to a color's frequency; Bruno hears them with an unsettling precision.
**Real meaning:** Bruno's hearing is anomalous (not a marrón's) — an early sensory tell of what he is. As the world drains, the true pitch fails; he alone hears the wrongness.
**Plant in:** 2
**Echo in:** 5, 6, 16 (one bell a touch off-tune; no one notices)
**Payoff in:** 19 (a cracked, flat hour-bell — the drained color stole its true pitch; Bruno hears the death of the note no one else can)
**Trigger:** the Aceleración draining the color that sustained the bell's frequency (intrinsic, world-level); Bruno's anomalous ear is the seeded reason he alone catches it.
**How to plant:** delight, then unease — he hears too well. Texture, not flag.
**How to pay off:** a small click — the flat bell ties his anomaly to the world's draining; he half-understands he is becoming a hueco. Do not explain the mechanism.
**Dose:** medium gap → plant + 3 echoes; the ch 16 echo (a bell a touch off) is the one quiet warning before the ch 19 payoff. Do not re-describe the tuning at ch 19's opening.
**Status:** planned

## SEED: lectura-al-nacer
**Detail:** the Reading rite — a prism held over a newborn's face decides marrón (ignored) / Blanco (claimed) / Negro (killed). Shown in a village in ch 3. (bible §14)
**Real meaning:** the gesture that decided Bruno's life 18 years ago (Vela marked him marrón — her failure). The reader must carry the gesture so ch 25 (and Libro III) land without exposition.
**Plant in:** 3
**Echo in:** 8 (Vela reading records — the same prism gesture in the archive)
**Payoff in:** Libro III (Vela reads Bruno; the reader recognizes the gesture). Libro-I touch: ch 25, Vela's definitive Reading of Bruno discharges the recognition.
**How to plant:** a full village rite in ch 3, liturgical and ordinary; the prism, the silence, the verdict. Not about Bruno.
**How to pay off:** when Vela performs the prism-Reading on Bruno (ch 25 / Libro III), the reader recognizes the gesture from ch 3 without being told. Let it click.
**Dose:** wide gap → plant once richly, echo once obliquely (ch 8). The power is in the reader's memory, not repetition.
**Status:** planned

## SEED: complementario-verde-magenta
**Detail:** a magenta-blooded man falters for an instant before a green woman in a market scuffle (ch 3); nobody understands it. (bible §14)
**Real meaning:** the verdad-arma — green in counter-phase cancels magenta; the lowest caste is the biological counter to the kings. The weapon of the green revolution.
**Plant in:** 3
**Echo in:** 10 (Yedra tests it small), 14 (on a person — a man falls, not yet dead), 18 (a magenta falters in the crowd again), 24 (Vela hears of the "death with no mark")
**Payoff in:** 22 (Yedra kills the magenta capataz by counter-frequency — first political assassination by complementario). Full payoff: Libro III (the revolution topples the Corona).
**Trigger:** ch 22 — the harvest/tax day (recurring event seeded ch 14) brings the capataz to the village + the hollow brother present (emotional trigger). Never a convenient stranger; the calendar of the expolio brings him.
**How to plant:** a beat inside a busy market — the stumble lasts an instant and is dismissed as a trip. No flag.
**How to pay off:** ch 22 — the magenta falls without a mark, in a silence that snuffs rather than strikes. The cost shows on Yedra (she becomes, a degree, the monster).
**Dose:** wide gap → plant + 4 escalating echoes (stumble → small test → a man falls → crowd echo → a kill). Each raises the stakes a step; never restate the physics in narration.
**Status:** planned

## SEED: hermano-hueco
**Detail:** Yedra's younger brother, milked by the ordeño and returned grey and hollow — alive but emptied — present at her table.
**Real meaning:** the human cost of the ordeño made flesh; Yedra's wound and the engine of her rage. Also a mirror of what Bruno is becoming inside (the grey).
**Plant in:** 3
**Echo in:** 10, 14
**Payoff in:** 22 (his hollow presence is the emotional trigger that lets Yedra go through with the kill)
**Trigger:** ch 22 — emotional, not mechanical: feeding her hollow brother on the morning of the cosecha hardens her hand.
**How to plant:** a body at the table who is fed and does not answer; the grey dust on his skin. Grief shown as routine, not lament.
**How to pay off:** ch 22 — she looks at him once before she acts; the reader knows why without a word.
**Dose:** medium gap → plant + 2 echoes. The hollow brother recurs as presence, never as speech.
**Status:** planned

## SEED: crisol-tensionado
**Detail:** the control-class's thermal sink / crucible (the safety net that drains excess heat) is shown jury-rigged and strained — propped, humming wrong under load.
**Real meaning:** the loaded gun of the midpoint. Its failure is what forces Bruno's first honest use; it must be on the page before it fails.
**Plant in:** 7
**Echo in:** 9 (shown straining in a demo), 11 (creaks a millimetre under rehearsal load)
**Payoff in:** 13 (it fails under the dorado's overload, forcing Bruno to pour the heat to the void)
**Trigger:** ch 13 — intrinsic: the dorado's seeded ambition pushes him past his limit on exam day + the already-strained sink (shown ch 7/9/11) gives way. No external cause arrives to break it; the thing the reader was shown failing is what fails.
**How to plant:** background apparatus of the control room — the propped strut, the off hum. Never "ominously." Just shown, used, strained.
**How to pay off:** ch 13 — it gives way at the worst moment; the reader has already heard it crack (ch 9 transition).
**Dose:** **near-term detonation** (plant ch 7, pays ch 13 — wide enough, but the ch 11 echo is close to payoff). Do NOT re-describe the strut at ch 13's opening; let the earlier creaks do the work. One quiet touch per appearance.
**Status:** planned

## SEED: mentor-cuaderno
**Detail:** the reformist mentor takes notes after every conversation with Bruno, measures him with prisms, keeps a notebook — attention Bruno reads as care.
**Real meaning:** the mentor sees Bruno as the proof of his reform thesis, not as a person; he is building a paper to publish. The all-is-lost betrayal.
**Plant in:** 2
**Echo in:** 6, 7 (Bruno glimpses him writing, misreads it as care), 11 (the thesis aired in the abstract), 13 (reads Bruno from afar as a case), 16 (sharpened attention; colloquium announced)
**Payoff in:** 20 (Bruno finds the drafts — his case as ideological proof; the mentor was publishing, not protecting)
**Trigger:** ch 20 — intrinsic: the colloquium/publication date (seeded ch 16) brings the papers out onto the study desk; Bruno's trusted access (the key, given ch 16) lets him in for an innocent reason. Nothing convenient.
**How to plant:** the notebook is a tic, not a clue — the gesture of a careful teacher. Let the reader, like Bruno, read it as devotion.
**How to pay off:** ch 20 — Bruno reads his own name turned into a variable; the betrayal is that the mentor believes he is being good. No villain reveal; a worse, quieter thing.
**Dose:** wide gap → plant + 5 light echoes, each as ordinary mentorly attention. The horror is retroactive at ch 20.
**Status:** planned

## SEED: vela-auditoria
**Detail:** Vela audits her own Readings from eighteen years ago and finds an anomaly: a "marrón" Reading whose frequencies don't sum.
**Real meaning:** that file is Bruno; Vela marked him marrón as a baby — her failure. The Aceleración made the old expedientes legible, so she can now resolve what she missed. Her line of the hunt.
**Plant in:** 8
**Echo in:** 12 (narrows the anomaly to Altaluz's region), 15 (the records arrive), 24 (records + legible anomaly + calibrated prism assembled)
**Payoff in:** 25 (she Reads Bruno with the calibrated prism and identifies him — Blanco × Negro; her 18-year-old failure has a face)
**Trigger:** ch 25 — intrinsic: the Aceleración (seeded as the world-drain) is what makes the old Reading legible and the present one decisive; her audit, not coincidence, brings her to Altaluz.
**How to plant:** procedural, cold — a discrepancy in an old ledger, the prism turned on a sealed record. Her dread is the texture, not exposition.
**How to pay off:** ch 25 — the definitive Reading; the gesture the reader knows from ch 3/8 clicks. Costs show as Vela's certainty-and-horror: a life of killing meant something and nothing.
**Dose:** wide gap → plant + 3 procedural echoes tightening the net. Never let Vela voice "the baby is alive and near" until the convergence earns it.
**Status:** planned

## SEED: anillo-del-sello
**Detail:** before any Reading, Vela turns the Orden del Sello's ring inward on her finger — a private professional rite. (bible §14)
**Real meaning:** marks the kind of person who hunts Bruno; in Libro III Bruno understands, too late, what the gesture meant. A loaded gun for the trilogy.
**Plant in:** 8
**Echo in:** 15, 24
**Payoff in:** Libro III (Bruno understands the gesture too late). Libro-I touch: ch 25, the gesture precedes the Reading that identifies him.
**How to plant:** a small, repeated motion before she reads — never explained. Texture of her hands.
**How to pay off:** (Libro III) recognition. In Libro I, the gesture simply recurs at the climax Reading, banking the image.
**Dose:** wide gap → plant + 2 echoes, identical motion each time (this is the rare seed where exact repetition *is* the point — it must be unmistakable later). Keep it brief.
**Status:** planned

## SEED: blanco-falso
**Detail:** the noble who overheats burns toward white before dying and is mistaken for ascending to sainthood; the political canonization of a cooked noble. (bible §14)
**Real meaning:** the same mechanism the system uses to consume its own and call it holy — and exactly what Vela will be willing to do to Bruno in Libro III.
**Plant in:** 9 (named as a school/liturgical warning) → in scene 13 (the dorado burning toward white = a Blanco Falso in miniature)
**Echo in:** 18
**Payoff in:** 15 (Vela canonizes the Portaluz's cooked cousin as a saint — the Blanco Falso dramatized in full). Full payoff: Libro III (Bruno realizes Vela would do the same to him).
**Trigger:** ch 13's near-miss is caused by the crisol failure (see crisol-tensionado); ch 15's canonization is a scheduled political rite, not a contrivance.
**How to plant:** ch 9 as a dry warning; ch 13 the reader *sees* a body burning toward white for the first time (the dorado, saved in time).
**How to pay off:** ch 15 — a cooked corpse displayed as relic, incense over the smell of burning, Vela presiding and knowing. The reader connects it to the dorado without being told.
**Dose:** medium → named (ch 9) → seen small (ch 13) → seen full (ch 15) → echo (ch 18). Escalation by scale, not repetition.
**Status:** planned

## SEED: primer-uso-honesto
**Detail:** Bruno pours his magic to the void openly for the first time, saving the dorado; the room changes visibly and the cost descolors him within. (bible §14)
**Real meaning:** the central demonstration of the inverted system — power that erases the user. Each honest use spends a luminous memory. The midpoint overturning.
**Plant in:** 13 (fires at the midpoint)
**Echo in:** (its cost echoes through madre-telar: 17, 19, 21)
**Payoff in:** Libro III (Bruno has emptied himself enough that the cicatriz can open). Libro-I discharge: ch 13 itself (the mother's voice goes) and ch 25 (the mother's face goes).
**Trigger:** ch 13 — the crisol failure + the dorado's thermal runaway force the use (intrinsic; see crisol-tensionado). At ch 25 the trigger is the flight from Vela.
**How to plant:** this is the midpoint event, not a background seed — but plant the *cost* as confusion, not understood loss. Bruno reaches for the song and finds its shape only.
**How to pay off:** (Libro III) the emptying is complete enough to open the cicatriz. In Libro I, the reader simply learns the rule: he pays in memory.
**Dose:** the use itself is loud (it must be — it's the midpoint); the *cost* is quiet and must not be explained. Show the gap, never narrate "he lost a memory."
**Status:** planned
