# Character arcs — El Apagado (Libro I)

> One section per principal. The arc is the private transformation
> separate from the plot. Read `references/fantasy-beats.md` (Character
> arcs section) for the structure.
>
> Wound → Want → Need → Lie → Decision → Transformation.
>
> Note (trilogy): Bruno's full positive arc consummates in Libro III; Vela's
> negative-to-tragic and Yedra's positive-to-tragic also complete later. The
> waypoints below are the **Libro I segment** of each.

## Bruno
- **Wound (still active):** a world that reduces everyone to their color; no one has ever looked at him as a person. He lived hidden before he knew what he was hiding (his dead mother's secret).
- **Want (conscious goal):** survive and belong.
- **Need (real healing):** to be seen as a person, not a function — and, the inversion, to *see* another as a person and choose them.
- **Lie they believe:** "valgo por lo que puedo hacer" (I'm worth what I can do).
- **Decision point (which chapter):** 25 — chooses to stay one more day to protect the mentor (who saw him as thesis), acting as a person, not a function. Aligns with the climax.
- **Transformation type:** positive (Libro I = the first active step; full consummation Libro III). In Libro I he moves from protective invisibility to being seen — and to his first chosen act.

### Waypoints
- **Act 1 — state at start:** invisible, hidden, valued by no one; the lie intact. Mother's memory whole and warm (ch 1-7).
- **First crack (ch ~11):** the mentor's attention makes him feel seen-as-person; the first faint cost to a luminous memory (erosion begins to sting).
- **Midpoint shift (ch 13):** first honest use; visible at last — but pays with his mother's voice. The lie ("worth = use") is reinforced even as the cost begins to undo him.
- **All-is-lost low (ch 20-21):** finds the mentor's drafts; understands he was seen as function even by the one who seemed to know him. The lie collapses; nothing is left to be valued for.
- **Decision moment (ch 25):** chooses to stay a day to protect the mentor — the first act as a person. Pays with his mother's face.
- **End-state:** alone in the world for the first time, descolorándose, hunted — but he has acted, once, as a person. Seed of the Libro III consummation.

## Vela
- **Wound (still active):** eighteen years ago she Read a Negro as marrón — let him pass. She didn't know then. Now the Aceleración makes the old files legible and the doubt corrodes her: decades of killing meant everything and nothing.
- **Want (conscious goal):** hunt the surviving Negro and repair her failure.
- **Need (real healing):** to learn that purity does not save. (She does NOT reach this in Libro I — reserved for Libros II-III.)
- **Lie they believe:** "soy lo único entre el mundo y el vacío" (I am the only thing between the world and the void).
- **Decision point (which chapter):** Libro I has no clean decision for her — her arc *deepens* rather than turns. The closest beat is ch 15 (she canonizes a Blanco Falso, refusing her need to keep standing) and ch 25 (she completes the hunt, ratifying her office).
- **Transformation type:** negative-to-tragic (deepens conviction in Libro I; pays the moral price later). In Libro I she hardens.

### Waypoints
- **Act 1 — state at start (ch 8):** certain, liturgical, a lifetime of Readings behind her; the doubt only just beginning to corrode.
- **First crack (ch 8):** the anomaly in her own archive — documentary proof of her failure. Certainty fractures.
- **Midpoint shift (ch 12):** narrows the anomaly to Altaluz's region; the abstract dread acquires a place. The hunt becomes concrete.
- **All-is-lost low (ch 15):** she canonizes a cooked noble she knows is a Blanco Falso — her need (purity doesn't save) brushes her and she refuses it to keep standing. Her lowest moral point, chosen.
- **Decision moment (ch 24-25):** she assembles the proof and performs the definitive Reading — ratifying her office, closing the circle she opened 18 years ago.
- **End-state:** she has a name, a face, and a day's lead. Convinced she is right (and the book agrees she has a case). The tragedy is set, not yet paid.

## Yedra
- **Wound (still active):** her younger brother was milked by the drenaje and returned grey and hollow — alive but emptied, at her table.
- **Want (conscious goal):** vengeance and justice for the huecos of her village.
- **Need (real healing):** to learn that winning demands not becoming the monster she fights.
- **Lie they believe:** "la fuerza basta" (force is enough).
- **Decision point (which chapter):** 22 — she kills the magenta capataz by counter-frequency; the first time she fully crosses from victim to perpetrator. (The *recognition* of the cost — her Need — is reserved for later books.)
- **Transformation type:** positive-to-tragic (in Libro I she gains power and crosses the line; she wins what she pursues at the cost of becoming someone she won't recognize — paid in Libro III).

### Waypoints
- **Act 1 — state at start (ch 3):** victim and witness; measures people by their hands; rage held quiet. The brother hollow at her table.
- **First crack (ch 3):** sees the magenta falter before a green and *files it* — the lie ("force is enough") meets an actual weapon.
- **Midpoint shift (ch 10):** tests the counter-phase small and recruits; moves from victim to organizer. The lie hardens into doctrine.
- **All-is-lost low / line crossed (ch 14):** drops a man at will for the first time (not yet a kill). She feels the edge of becoming the monster and justifies it with rage.
- **Decision moment (ch 22):** kills the capataz on harvest day, her hollow brother present. Wins — and the victory tastes of ash. A new weight in her hands.
- **End-state:** an organizer with a proven weapon and a first death on her hands; the Corona can't name how he fell. The tragic cost is seeded, not yet collected.

## Subplot contact points (≥3 touches with the main arc)

- **Subplot A (Yedra / green revolution):** Touch 1 — Bruno's province and Yedra's share the drenaje's grey (ch 1/3, thematic mirror: Bruno greys inside as the huecos grey outside). Touch 2 — the verdad-arma (complementario) and Bruno's anomaly are the two things the system cannot read; both surface as the world drains (Aceleración as common engine). Touch 3 — the climax: Yedra's first counter-frequency kill (ch 22) and Bruno's first chosen act (ch 25) land together, both answering "is force enough? / what is a person worth?" from opposite sides. The death "with no mark" (ch 22) reaches Vela (ch 24), braiding the threads.
- **Subplot B (Portaluz's rise):** mentioned from ch 1 (coin, shrines), in scene ch 12, apotheosis ch 18. Touches the main arc as the decoy that draws the world's eye while the real danger (Bruno) and the real weapon (the contra) work in shadow. Resolves open in Libro I (the Portaluz stands, uncooked) — deliberately, with thematic resonance for Libro III.
